How Elections Work in India

A simple, step-by-step overview of the election process.

  1. 1

    Voter Registration

    Citizens aged 18+ apply online at voters.eci.gov.in using Form 6. Proof of age and address are required. A Booth Level Officer (BLO) verifies the details.

  2. 2

    Verification & Voter ID

    After verification, your name is added to the electoral roll and you receive an EPIC (Voter ID) card. This can also be downloaded digitally.

  3. 3

    Election Announcement

    The Election Commission announces election dates. The Model Code of Conduct comes into effect. Voter slips are distributed with booth details.

  4. 4

    Voting Day

    Voters go to their designated polling booth with a valid ID. They press the EVM button for their candidate. A VVPAT slip confirms the vote for 7 seconds.

  5. 5

    Counting & Result

    Votes are counted at designated centers under strict security. Results are declared by the Returning Officer. The party/candidate with the most votes wins.

Voter Helpline: Call 1950 (toll-free) for any election-related help.

For registration, you need:

  • Proof of age — Aadhaar, birth certificate, 10th marksheet, or passport
  • Proof of address — electricity bill, water bill, bank passbook, or rent agreement
  • Passport-size photograph

On voting day, you can use any one of 12 approved IDs: Voter ID, Aadhaar, Passport, Driving License, PAN Card, and more.

If you have moved to a different constituency, fill Form 6 at your new address. If you moved within the same constituency, fill Form 8A.

  • Submit proof of new address (Aadhaar / utility bill / rent agreement)
  • Your name will be transferred to the new voter list within 30–45 days
  • You can only vote in the constituency where you are currently registered
