📋 About Elections

What Are Elections?

Democracy is built on the power of your vote. Here's why elections matter and how you fit in.

🏛️

Democracy's Foundation

Elections are the cornerstone of democracy — they give citizens the power to choose their representatives and hold them accountable. India is the world's largest democracy with 970+ million voters.

⚖️

Article 326 — Right to Vote

The Indian Constitution guarantees every citizen aged 18+ the right to vote (Universal Adult Suffrage). This right is equal regardless of religion, caste, gender, or economic status.

🗳️

Your Vote = Your Voice

Many elections in India are won by margins of fewer than 200 votes. Your single vote genuinely changes outcomes. It's not just a right — it's your most powerful civic tool.

📋

Types of Elections

India holds Lok Sabha (national), Vidhan Sabha (state), and local body elections. The Election Commission of India conducts all of them independently.

🔐

Secret Ballot Guaranteed

Your vote is completely anonymous. No one — not the government, not election officials — can ever find out who you voted for. This is protected by law.

🌐

The Election Commission

An autonomous constitutional authority, the ECI operates independently from the government. It ensures free and fair elections through strict monitoring and enforcement of the Model Code of Conduct.
